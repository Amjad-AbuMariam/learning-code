1-3 — Flexbox

~60 minutes · Week 1, Day 1 · Source: curriculum Flexbox.md

Why this matters: Flexbox is the workhorse layout tool for every page you'll build in this course, starting with the profile page from lesson 1-2.

Idea 1 — A container and its items, on two axes

Flexbox has exactly two roles: the flex container (the parent you give display: flex) and its flex items (the direct children, which the container arranges). The container lays items along its main axis — horizontal by default, set by flex-direction — and the cross axis runs perpendicular to it.

Idea 2 — One property per question

QuestionProperty (on the container)
Which direction do items flow?flex-direction: row | column (+ -reverse variants)
How is space distributed along the main axis?justify-content: flex-start | center | flex-end | space-between | space-around | space-evenly
How do items align on the cross axis?align-items: stretch | flex-start | center | flex-end | baseline
May items wrap to more lines?flex-wrap: nowrap | wrap

And one property on an item: flex-grow: 1 lets that item take a share of leftover space — all items with flex-grow: 1 share it equally. The remaining item properties (order, flex-shrink, align-self, align-content for multi-line containers) are on the cheat sheet — look up, don't memorize.

Prove it to yourself

Change one dropdown at a time and predict the result before it renders. In particular: set align-items: center, then flip flex-direction to column — watch which way "center" moves. The main axis follows the direction; justify-content follows the main axis.

Do it for real (~30 min)

Continue in ~/code/week-1/index.html from lesson 1-2. Add a <style> block inside <head>.

  1. Make the nav a row with breathing room: 
    nav { display: flex; gap: 1rem; }   /* row with breathing room */
    Save, refresh. You should see the three links side by side with even spacing.
  2. Push the nav links to the right edge: add this to the same rule. Refresh. 
    justify-content: flex-end;   /* push links to the right */
    You should see them hug the right side.
  3. Give the two project articles a shared container by wrapping them in this (inside the projects section). Refresh — nothing visibly changes yet; that's expected. 
    <div class="cards">
</div>
  4. Lay the cards out: 
    .cards { display: flex; gap: 1rem; flex-wrap: wrap; }   /* cards share a row and wrap */
.cards article { flex-grow: 1; border: 1px solid #ccc; padding: 1rem; }   /* cards grow and get borders */
    Refresh. You should see two bordered cards side by side.
  5. Shrink the browser window slowly. Success check: when the window gets narrow, the cards wrap into a single column — that's flex-wrap working; you'll control it precisely in lesson 1-6.
  6. Optional but excellent practice: play Flexbox Froggy (from the curriculum's Day 1 schedule) up to at least level 13.

Check yourself — predict the output

Without touching the playground: a row-direction container has justify-content: space-between and three items. Where does the free space go?

Reveal

Between the items only: first item flush left, last item flush right, equal gaps between neighbors — no space at the container's edges. (space-around would also pad the edges by half a gap.)
